About The Wheelchair OTs

Who we are

The Wheelchair OTs was founded by Matt Wellman and Sally Lindberg, registered OT professionals who share a passion for providing comprehensive, compassionate and client-centred care. Our joint goal is maximising our clients’ independence and quality of life through optimising their mobility, support and comfort.

We have a combined 24 years of experience in Occupational Therapy, specialising in the field of posture and seating. We have brought together our knowledge and skills of working in the NHS and private sector to develop a comprehensive, easy to access wheelchair assessment service for both individuals and professionals.

Meet the Team

Our expert therapists, Matt and Sally, have over 24 years of combined experience in supporting people with limited mobility to live more independently. You can find out more about them below.

Matt Wellman, BSc

Matt is an Occupational Therapist with over 10 years working experience in the health and social care sector. Matt is HCPC registered and initially worked for a local authority supporting people with long term health conditions in the community. This role further developed Matt’s skills in moving and handling tasks as well as building adaptations to support and promote independence.

Since 2016, Matt has worked in wheelchair services in the Southeast and London supporting children and adults with wheelchair provision. Matt has extensive knowledge on a wide range of equipment to meet the individual’s needs.

Matt’s passion is working with people to identify what is important to them. Working together, to maximise their mobility and guiding them using his clinical experience to products that will provide the best possible solution.

Sally Lindberg MA, Dip COT

Sally is an HCPC Registered Occupational Therapist with over 15 years’ experience in NHS, Social Services and the charitable sector. She is experienced in working with people with a range of neurological and orthopaedic conditions, including acquired brain injury, spinal cord injury, amputation and osteo/rheumatoid arthritis.

Since 2017, Sally has worked as a Wheelchair Therapist in the NHS, carrying out clinical assessments and providing tailored wheelchair and equipment solutions for clients in the community. She also runs training programmes in posture and pressure management for other OT’s/ healthcare professionals.

Sally is passionate about enabling people to achieve their personal, mobility and lifestyle goals. By drawing upon her wealth of clinical experience and product knowledge, Sally supports clients to identify their needs and will recommend the best equipment/ solutions to maximise their independence and comfort.
