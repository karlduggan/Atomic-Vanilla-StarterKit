Frequently asked questions

There are many benefits to being assessed by The Wheelchair OTs. You will be assessed by an experienced Occupational Therapist who will be able to discuss solutions to your mobility needs ensuring that products recommended are suitable for your long-term needs. We also consider the needs of family members and friends to ensure the equipment is suitable for them to manage moving forward. There are many factors that come into our thinking before arriving at a product that is suitable and in turn successful to meet your desired outcomes. At The Wheelchair OTs we have experience of supporting individuals with minimal to complex postural and positioning needs. This understanding and knowledge means we can recommend wheelchairs that provide comfort and support a posture to enable as much function as possible, thus enabling the individual to be as engaged with activity as possible. We will also offer advice to ensure you are not ‘over sold’ products. By understanding the products and costs, we will be able to ensure that you do not overspend on equipment that is not needed, saving you money.
No, we are not a mobility dealership, so we will not be selling you equipment directly. This is important as it enables us to operate with impartiality. Following assessment, we will provide you with a prescription form for a wheelchair that we would recommend and will discuss where these can be purchased from.
Following the assessment, you will be provided with a summary of needs report including recommendations of equipment provision. Ordinarily we would not follow-up after this, but if more sessions were desired this is something that can be discussed at any time during the process.
We can offer an appointment within days of you contacting The Wheelchair OTs. There are no long waiting lists and work can be undertaken promptly to understand your individual needs.
We currently cover the Southeast of England. If you are outside this area, please do contact us as we may be able to offer a service still.
Following the appointment, you can expect a copy of your summary of needs from the therapist. You will also be provided with written recommendations (if equipment provision is required), which will enable you to go with confidence to a mobility dealer, knowing the equipment will be most suitable to meet your identified needs.
